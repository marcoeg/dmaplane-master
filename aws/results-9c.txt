NOT deleted by CloudFormation:

AMI (ami-0960d74ca58008230) — AMIs are never in the stack, you created it manually during 9B. It persists until you deregister it.
EBS snapshots backing the AMI — also persist independently.

The AMI + snapshot cost is negligible (~$0.05/GB/month for the snapshot, probably ~$1/month). Keep them if you plan to redeploy for the blog cleanup run. Delete when done:
bash# When you're truly done:
aws ec2 deregister-image --region us-west-2 --image-id ami-0960d74ca58008230

# Find and delete the backing snapshot:
aws ec2 describe-snapshots --region us-west-2 --owner-ids self \
  --query "Snapshots[?Description.contains(@,'ami-0960d74ca58008230')].SnapshotId" --output text
# Then:
aws ec2 delete-snapshot --region us-west-2 --snapshot-id <snap-xxx>
